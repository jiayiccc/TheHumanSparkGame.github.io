# Human Spark: Martian Selection System

**Human Spark** is an interactive experience that transforms human behavior into dynamic “Martian” personality profiles. Players engage with a series of tasks and challenges, and their choices and reactions are used to generate unique behavioral profiles represented as Martian lifeforms.

## Overview

In a near-future scenario, humans are selected for migration to Mars. Traditional labels are insufficient to capture the complexity of human behavior. **Human Spark** captures behavior in real time and converts it into a Martian personality, offering a new perspective on identity assessment.

---

## Gameplay

* Players navigate interactive tasks that capture decision-making, reaction time, and interaction patterns.
* Behavioral data is recorded continuously without interrupting the experience.
* The system synthesizes actions into a visualized Martian lifeform profile.
* Profiles can be downloaded or shared as images.

---

## System

* **Data Capture:** Reaction times, choices, and interaction patterns.
* **Mapping:** Behavioral signals are translated into underlying personality dimensions.
* **Profile Generation:** Dimensions are combined to create a unique, dynamic Martian personality.

---

## Design & Interface

* Interactive elements respond in real time to user input.
* Particle systems and motion-based feedback reflect behavioral patterns.
* Visual design emphasizes continuous engagement rather than segmented tasks.

---

## Iteration & Validation

* Conducted over 30+ user testing cycles.
* Surveys and feedback informed adjustments to behavioral dimensions and classification logic.
* Iterative design ensured profiles are meaningful and consistent.

---

## Outcome

* Users receive a personalized Martian personality profile.
* Each profile is a synthesized identity with distinct traits and tendencies.
* Profiles are downloadable and shareable as images.

---

## Potential

* Extends beyond game context to real-world applications such as recruiting, assessment, and behavior-based matching.
* Offers a behavior-driven framework for evaluating individuals without relying on static labels.
* Suggests new approaches to identity construction in future environments.
